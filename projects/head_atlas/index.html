Head Atlas

NLP Research Feb 2026 transformers pytorch huggingface SOMP

Overview

This project started with the goal of replicating the findings of the HeadPursuit paper, which explores how individual attention heads in transformer models specialize in different linguistic tasks. From there, the work extended in three directions.

What was done

  • Replication of the original HeadPursuit findings on standard benchmark models.
  • Generalization of the interpretability method to any model available on HuggingFace, removing the original paper's constraint to a fixed set of architectures.
  • Investigation of attention head specialization metrics and their potential use as a stopping criterion for the SOMP (Simultaneous Orthogonal Matching Pursuit) algorithm.
  • Visualization of attention heads organized by their specialization metric, enabling intuitive comparison of head behavior across layers and models.

Motivation

Understanding what individual attention heads "do" is a central problem in transformer interpretability. Most existing tools either require custom training procedures or are tied to specific model families. By decoupling the method from the architecture, this project makes head analysis accessible to the broader NLP ecosystem.

Technical approach

The approach uses probing tasks to assign specialization scores to each attention head. These scores are then fed into a sparse selection algorithm (SOMP) to identify which heads are most informative for a given linguistic property. The stopping criterion investigation asks: can we use the rate of change of specialization scores to decide when to stop adding heads, rather than relying on a fixed budget?
